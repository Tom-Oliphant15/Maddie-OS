## Audience

### Primary audience

Adults aged 25 to 45, mixed female and male. They live in and around Leamington Spa, Warwick, and Kenilworth. Professionally doing well — teachers, nurses, office managers, marketing people, small business owners. Not wealthy, but they spend money on things that matter to them. They probably have gym memberships, hobbies outside work, and they do parkrun on a Saturday. Family situation varies, but many are juggling work and kids, which means their own health often comes last. Small percentage of elite athletes; a lot of hobbyists who hurt themselves by moving wrong or pushing too much.

### Secondary audiences

- **The performance-focused amateur athlete** (25 to 40, male). Goes to the gym regularly. Plays football, rugby, or golf at the weekend. Takes his training seriously even though it's amateur level. Comes to F&E not because something is broken, but because he wants to perform better. Wants S&C sessions, sports massage as maintenance, keeping his body in good shape. Proactive rather than reactive. Likely crossover audience with Fade Golf.
- **The post-injury returner** (30 to 50, mixed gender). Has had a significant injury, an operation, a long-term absence from sport, or a serious muscle/joint issue. Has been through NHS physio but wants more. Comes to F&E for the next stage.

### What makes them book / buy / sign up (in their own words)

- "This knee isn't getting better on its own"
- "I've got that 10k in 8 weeks I can't just keep ignoring it"
- "I keep getting the same injury, there must be a reason"
- "I want to get back to where I was before, not just functional"
- "physiotherapy Warwickshire"
- "sports massage Leamington Spa"
- "deep tissue massage Leamington"

### What stops them booking

- "What if they tell me something I don't want to hear"
- "I don't even know if I need a physio or a sports therapist or a massage, I don't know the difference"
- "I don't want to be told to stop training"
- "What if I'm not ready and I make it worse"

### Where they hang out online

- **Instagram** — primary platform across audiences. Scrolling in the evening after work. Follow fitness accounts, local businesses, maybe a running account or two.
- **Facebook** — relevant for Leamington specifically. Local community groups are massive: Leamington Spa Noticeboard, Warwick and Leamington community pages. This is where the 35-50 age group lives. Events, recommendations, word of mouth all happen here.
- **Google** — not social, but the most important. When someone's knee hurts at 11pm they're not on Instagram, they're on Google. High-intent traffic comes from here.
- Local Leamington / Warwick fitness studios and gyms, running accounts, Runna, Parkrun, Couch to 5K, lifestyle and wellness accounts, local food and lifestyle accounts.

### Where they hang out offline

- Local gyms
- Yoga and Pilates studios around Leamington and Warwick
- Swimming clubs and leisure centres
- Independent coffee shops in Leamington, Warwick, and Kenilworth high streets (for the slightly older audience)
- Local Facebook community groups that translate into real-life meetups
