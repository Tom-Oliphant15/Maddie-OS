## Audience

Stride is multi-service. Audiences answered across the services, with overlaps noted.

### Primary audience (across services)

25 to 80, even split men and women. Four overlapping segments:

- **Working adults** who need to get back to work or stay active
- **Retired older people** affected by mobility issues
- **Parents** of younger patients (10 to 18) who are embarrassed or in pain
- **Independent older adults** who are struggling with mobility

### Secondary audiences

- **Sporty men 18 to 40** — runners, footballers, gym-goers who have a deadline (a race, a match, a competition) pushing them to finally do something about it. Pain tolerance is higher, so they have often left it longer than they should.
- **Women pre-summer** — less specific to nail surgery, but worth noting from the Routine Podiatry profile. Women who want their feet sorted before sandal season. Overlaps with nail surgery audience when an ingrown toenail is part of a broader foot-care concern.

### What makes them book / buy / sign up (in their own words)

- "This has been going on for over a week now, something's not right"
- "It's gone red and started swelling, I'm worried it's infected"
- "Ingrown toenail won't go away"
- "Ingrown toenail treatment near me"

### What stops them booking

- "Is it going to hurt?"
- "I don't know what nail surgery actually involves"
- "My feet are disgusting, I don't want anyone looking at them"
- "I'll just try and sort it myself first"

### Where they hang out online

- **Google Search** — primary channel, which is exactly why the per-service Google Ads restructure matters
- **Facebook** — particularly the older audience (45+) and parents
- **Instagram** — younger active audience, sporty 18-35
- **Local community Facebook groups** — Leamington, Warwick, Kenilworth
- **Running clubs and local sport teams** (online presence)
- **General health and wellbeing accounts**
- **Local parenting groups** (for parents booking for their child)
- Search behaviour in groups: "Recommendations for a podiatrist in Leamington" / "Has anyone used Stride Clinic?" type posts

### Where they hang out offline

- Local markets and high streets in Leamington and Warwick
- Community centres, particularly for the older audience
- Church and social groups for the 65+ demographic
- Local running clubs (Leamington, Warwick, Kenilworth)
- Football, rugby and cricket clubs across Warwickshire
- Local gyms and leisure centres
- Golf clubs (audience crossover with Fade Golf)

### What they type into Google

- "ingrown toenail won't go away"
- "how do I know if my ingrown toenail is infected"
- "ingrown toenail treatment near me"
- "podiatrist near me ingrown toenail"

- "can I play football with an ingrown toenail"
