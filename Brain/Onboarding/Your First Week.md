# Maddie OS, Your First Week

*One task per day. By Friday you've used the OS end-to-end. Tick each off as you go. Living version of the printed guide in `Assets/`. Read it anytime by saying "first week", "what should I do this week", or "/help first week".*

---

## ☐ Monday: Greet the OS

**What to say:** `morning`

**Why:** First habit of the day. The whole OS hangs off this. Daily Briefing pulls your diary, emails, Slack, and your top three priorities into one snapshot so you know what today is about before opening Slack.

**Time:** 5 minutes

**You'll know it worked when:** you see your day laid out in one screen instead of three apps.

---

## ☐ Tuesday: Draft one piece of real work

**What to say:** `draft an email to [person] about [topic]` OR `expand campaign for [topic]`

**Why:** Get past the "I should just write it myself" reflex. The OS gets you to a first draft fast. You edit from there, you don't start from blank.

**Time:** 15 minutes

**You'll know it worked when:** you have a first draft in less time than it would take to start one cold.

---

## ☐ Wednesday: Run a quality check before publishing

**What to say:** `is this ready to ship` (then drop the asset)

**Why:** Pre-publish Check is your gate. It runs Proofread + Tone Check + Visual Brand Check on the right parts and gives one verdict. Skip it once and you'll keep skipping it.

**Time:** 5 minutes per check

**You'll know it worked when:** something gets flagged that you would have missed. Always does. That's the value.

---

## ☐ Thursday: Pressure-test something before you commit

**What to say:** `grill me on this`

**Why:** Pick something you're about to commit to (a shoot, an ambassador ask, a campaign direction). Grill Me asks the hard questions you wouldn't ask yourself. Your tendency is to agree rather than push back, so this does the pushing for you.

**Time:** 15 minutes

**You'll know it worked when:** you find at least one hole in your plan you hadn't seen. Fix it before you commit.

---

## ☐ Friday: Close the week

**What to say:** `weekly report` then `done`

**Why:** End your first week with a written summary of what landed and what slipped. Weekly Slack Report Draft writes it; you edit and post to `#fade-group-marketing`. Then Session End wraps the week, saves Brain updates, pushes to GitHub.

**Time:** 20 minutes

**You'll know it worked when:** Tom and Harry see your first weekly post in Slack. You've used the OS end-to-end.

---

## If you get stuck

Stuck at any point this week? Say `/help` and describe the job. If something looks broken, say `/diagnostics`. If you still can't move forward, ping Tom in Slack `#leadership` with one line on what you tried.

**Reminder:** You're not expected to use every skill in your first week. Five tasks. Done.
