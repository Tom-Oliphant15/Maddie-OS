# Fire & Earth Leamington — Voice

*STATUS: Live. Populated from Maddie OS Bootstrap Form (Maddie, 21 May 2026). Reviewed in-person 22 May.*

## Tone

Premium, considered, grounded, accessible. Not athlete-only. Not over-excited or laddy.

Brand statement (Maddie's words):

> You don't have to be an athlete to feel better in your body. Fire & Earth, Leamington Spa. Whether you're in pain, recovering, or just ready to feel like yourself again, Fire & Earth is here. We help people feel better. Properly better. Fire & Earth, Leamington Spa.

## Vocabulary

**Use:**
- Recover properly
- Feel like yourself again
- Relief
- In good hands
- At your own pace
- Highly qualified therapists
- Back to yourself
- Pain free
- Move better

**Avoid:**
- Bespoke
- Smash your goals
- Book now to avoid disappointment
- Life-changing
- Sports massage (rebrand to deep tissue in motion)
- For athletes
- For runners
- Quick fix
- Generic wellness platitudes ("self-care", "treat yourself") unless used pointedly

## Sentence Rhythm

Considered, not breathless. No double exclamation marks. No "Hey guys" openers. No all-caps for emphasis. No emoji clusters. Read like a credible sports therapy clinic, not a personal Instagram account.

## Bad Examples (drift catchers)

- *"Ready to SMASH your recovery goals? 💪 Our sports massage therapists are here to help you crush it and get back out there STRONGER than ever. Book now and don't miss out!"* — aggressive, exclamation-heavy, all-caps, emoji-heavy. Feels like a budget gym advert. Completely at odds with the premium positioning. Cheapens the brand instantly.
- *"Hey guys! 👋 So we've got some AMAZING news… our new physios have just joined the fam and honestly they are absolutely incredible, we are so so excited for you all to meet them!! 🙌🙌"* — over-excited, unprofessional, undermines the premium positioning. "Fam", "so so excited", double exclamation marks, hand-clap emojis. Reads like a personal Instagram, not a credible therapy clinic.
