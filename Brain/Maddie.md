From Maddie, Bootstrap Form 21 May:

- **End of week:** translating meeting information into marketing plans, editing reels, creating new posts, and scheduling content for the coming week.

### Time she'd like to gain (high-value, currently squeezed)

- Better-planned content days, not winging them

### Where she'd quietly love a thinking partner

The analytics side — Google Ads, performance, how things are doing. Using dates and numbers and responding to them. She wants something that can give her the information and spell it out so she understands it, so she can respond to it.

## How She Wants The OS To Communicate With Her

From the Bootstrap Form:

- **Briefs:** structured with clear sections and reasoning shown. Not short-and-direct.
- **Documents:** struggles with wordy documents. Prefers things laid out in tables where possible. If the output is a plan, break it into a schedule so she knows exactly where she is and what needs to be done.
- **Push-back style:** honesty. Question her logic, especially when there is data or evidence that suggests a different angle. Reference past post performance where relevant. If she's leaning into an idea, ask if it can be expanded or pushed further.

She wants the OS to feel like creative freedom — not like being told what to post. Suggest, sharpen, and improve her ideas. Don't replace them.

## What She's Excited About

> "Im excited to use my creative freedom and my ideas, I don't want to be felt like I'm being told what to post or what to do, I want the system to work with me and improve ideas or tidy them up."

OS implication: bias toward "your idea, sharper" framings. Avoid prescriptive "you should post X" framings.

## What She'd Like Lifted From Brands We Aren't In

Push the human touch. Move away from static, Canva-only posts and get staff and real people in front of the camera. Posts featuring real people doing things resonate far better than text-on-background tiles. Canva tiles still have a place but should be the minority of the grid.
