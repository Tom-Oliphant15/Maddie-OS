## Example: June 2026 report (abbreviated)

```markdown
# Social Trends, June 2026

## Headline

Short-form video continues to dominate, with 15 to 30 second clips winning across Reels and TikTok. The biggest shift this month is platform algorithms rewarding "watch-through" over "views", meaning the first 1 to 2 seconds of a clip matters more than the headline copy. For Maddie's three brands, this means hook discipline on video specifically (not just captions) becomes the primary creative lever.

## What is working

### Clinical / Health (Stride)
1. **Patient education videos with named clinician on camera outperform polished brand content by 3 to 5x.** Trust signal: real human in the clinical setting.
   - Why relevant: Wendy + Helen are the brand's most credible faces. Use them more often, not less.
   - How to test: next Stride campaign brief, replace stock-pattern hero shot with Wendy doing a 30-second "what most people try first that does not work" explainer.
   - Source: [Inbeat Agency analysis of UK private healthcare social, May 2026]

2. **"Day in the life of a [clinician]" format is having a moment** across podiatry, physio, dental social accounts.
   - Why: humanises the clinic, low-production-value works, drives saves.
   - How to test: 60-second day-in-life from Wendy. Phone-shot fine.

### Golf / Sport (Fade Golf)
1. **Equipment-focused content has dropped sharply in engagement** vs experience/lifestyle content.
   - Why: members buy a club not a product. Lifestyle is the brand.
   - How to test: Luke ambassador rollout already aligned with this.

### Wellness (Fire & Earth)
1. **Atmosphere-first imagery outperforms people-first imagery** in coworking + retreat verticals.
   - Why: the audience is buying the space, not the people.
   - How to test: F&E grid should re-balance toward "the room" shots.

## Platform-level updates

| Platform | Update | Impact | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Algorithm now weights saves >2x likes | Maddie's post structure should explicitly invite saves ("save this for the next time...") | Immediate |
| TikTok | Comment-driven content boost | "Comment YES" CTA pattern earning algorithmic lift | Immediate, test on next Stride campaign |

## What to test in the next campaign brief

1. **First 1 to 2 seconds of every video as a dedicated hook unit** (not the headline opener)
2. **Save-invitation CTAs on Instagram** ("save this for next time")
3. **Comment-driven TikTok CTAs**

## What is starting to feel old

- Generic "before-after" carousel pattern (saturated since March)
- Generic "team member intro" Reels (saturated since February)
```
