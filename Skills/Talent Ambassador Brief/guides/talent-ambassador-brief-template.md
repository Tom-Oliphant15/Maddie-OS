# Guide: Talent Ambassador Brief Template

The full output template used by [[Talent Ambassador Brief]]. Loaded only when the skill is invoked. Extracted from the skill file 2026-05-21 to keep the skill lean while preserving the full brief structure.

The Disclosure block branches across the six commercial-relationship tiers defined in [[talent-ambassador-brief-disclosure-tiers]]. Pick ONE branch based on Step 1.3 of the skill; do not include the others in the final output.

---

```markdown
# Brief for [Talent], [Brand], [Topic]

*Hey [Talent],*

*Quick brief for the [topic] post you are sharing for us. One page, easy to skim.*

---

## What we are asking

[2 to 3 sentences in plain English. What is the post, what should it accomplish, by when.]

## Why this matters

[1 to 2 sentences. Why this specific post, why now, why YOU specifically (the ambassador angle, the credibility, the audience reach).]

## The content

[Link to the footage / image / asset]

[If filming was done: 1 line on what is in the cut.]

## Example caption (in your voice, feel free to rewrite)

> [Draft caption in talent's likely voice, not brand voice. Conversational. Personal. 80 to 150 words for IG, 50 to 80 for TikTok.]
>
> [Include the brand mention naturally, not as a tag-only or as a brochure line.]

## Hashtags

**Use these (always):** #[brand handle], #[location], #[campaign hashtag if any]
**Add if it fits:** #[topic-specific hashtag 1], #[topic-specific hashtag 2]
**Mix in your own audience hashtags too** if you have ones that work for your following.

## Disclosure (this section is mandatory and branches on commercial relationship)

**Paid ambassador / contracted promo:**
> This is a paid partnership under UK ASA / CAP rules. Three things to do:
> 1. **In the caption, FIRST line, FIRST few words:** put `#ad` (clearest, works everywhere). `#paidpartnership` is also accepted. Must be visible BEFORE the "more" cut, not buried at the end of hashtags.
> 2. **Use the platform-native Paid Partnership label** in the upload flow on Instagram (or TikTok's Branded Content toggle on TikTok). This auto-discloses across feed, story, and Reels views and is the strongest defensible position if anyone queries.
> 3. **For Reels / TikTok / short-form video:** disclosure ALSO needs to appear IN the video itself (text overlay across the first second or two, or a verbal "this is an ad" callout). Caption-only disclosure has been ruled inadequate by ASA for short-form video.
>
> Do NOT use `#sp`, `#spon`, `#collab`, `#partner`, `#thanks [Brand]`, `#BrandAmbassador`, or `#[Brand]Ambassador` as your disclosure. ASA has explicitly ruled all of these inadequate.
>
> **12-month tail:** if you come off the ambassador retainer at some point and still post about us in the next 12 months, please keep disclosing. ASA treats lingering brand associations as still commercial for up to a year after the formal relationship ends.

**Gifted product or service (no fee, just the gift):**
> Even though there's no money changing hands, the gift creates a commercial relationship under CAP. Same caption position as paid (first line, first few words), but use the layered label `Ad - Gifted` (or `#ad #gifted` together). `#gifted` alone is NOT a sufficient disclosure.
>
> Short-form video: in-video text overlay still required (`Ad - Gifted` across the first second or two).

**Affiliate code or affiliate link:**
> If you're using a discount code (e.g. `LUKE10`) or affiliate link that earns you commission, that's a commercial relationship. Disclose at the SAME placement as the code or link:
> - Code or link in caption -> `#ad` at the start of the caption
> - Code or link spoken / shown in video -> `Ad` text overlay across the relevant section
> - Link in TikTok bio with a "link in bio" call-out -> `#ad` in the caption AND in-video overlay
>
> Affiliate disclosure follows the same regulatory frame as paid ambassador; ASA does not treat affiliate commission as a lesser tier.

**Employee posting brand content:**
> You work with us, so include that fact visibly in the caption (e.g. "I'm part of the team at [Brand]"). No `#ad` needed because the employment relationship is the disclosure, but it has to be obvious to a casual viewer reading the first line.
>
> Short-form video: the employment statement should also appear in-video (overlay or verbal) so a viewer who never reads the caption still gets it.

**Friend of the brand / no payment / no gifted product / no code:**
> No statutory disclosure needed. We'd still suggest a transparent line like "I'm a member here" or "I go to F&E" so it doesn't read as a stealth promo. No `#ad`.

**Patient sharing their treatment story (Stride):**
> This is your story to tell in your own words. Make sure: (a) you have a signed Patient Consent for this post on file with us, (b) you are comfortable with the level of identification (face, name, treatment area), (c) you can pull the post any time within 30 days by messaging admin@strideclinics.co.uk and we will remove it from our channels too.

## Things to avoid

- [Specific phrases or angles that would be off-brand, in plain English]
- [Anything that risks compliance for Stride: medical claims like "cures" / "permanent fix" / "guaranteed", before-after without patient consent, etc.]

## When to post

**Suggested:** [day + rough time slot, with reasoning: e.g. "Wed evening because membership audience is browsing"]
**Hard avoid:** [if there is a date or time to NOT post, e.g. "not Sunday because we are running another campaign"]

## Anything else

- [Maddie's contact for questions on the day]
- [If anyone needs to be tagged, list with handles]
- [If this is part of a series, mention the next post in the sequence]

*Thanks [Talent]. Shout if anything is unclear.*

*Maddie*

---

*Generated by Talent/Ambassador Brief skill. Source files: [brand Brain], [voice file], [campaign brief if any].*
```
