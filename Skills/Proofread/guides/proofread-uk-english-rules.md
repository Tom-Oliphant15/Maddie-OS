# Guide: Proofread UK English Rules

UK English baseline reference used by [[Proofread]]. Extracted from the skill file 2026-05-21 to keep the skill lean. The four-pass check (spelling, grammar, punctuation, stylistic) runs against these rules.

---

## Spelling (-ise / -our / -re / -mme)

- `organise / recognise / realise / analyse` (NOT organize, recognize, etc.)
- `colour / favour / behaviour / labour / honour` (NOT color, favor, etc.)
- `centre / theatre / metre / litre` (NOT center, theater, etc.)
- `programme` for events/TV (NOT program, except for software)
- `defence / offence / licence` (noun, with -ce) vs `license` (verb)
- `practice` (noun) vs `practise` (verb)
- `enquiry` (general) vs `inquiry` (formal investigation)
- `judgement` (UK) vs `judgment` (US, accepted in legal UK)
- `aluminium` (NOT aluminum)
- `whilst` is acceptable in UK English but `while` is fine

## Spelling (single vs double consonant)

- `travelled / cancelled / labelled / modelled` (UK doubles the L)
- `focused / focusing` is correct in both UK and US (NOT focussed in modern UK)

## Grammar

- "Different **from**" not "different than"
- "Compared **with**" (for analysis) or "compared **to**" (for likeness)
- Collective nouns can take plural: "the team are training" is acceptable UK English
- "Have got" (UK) vs "have gotten" (US, less common in UK)
- Past participle "got" not "gotten" in most cases

## Punctuation

- **NO em dashes** or **en dashes**, hard rule from CLAUDE.md, mangles when pasted between apps
- Single quotes for primary quotation in UK convention, though double is widely tolerated
- Full stops INSIDE quotation marks when the quotation is a complete sentence, OUTSIDE for fragments
- Oxford comma is optional in UK English (be consistent within a document)
- Date format: `2 June 2026` or `2nd June 2026`, NOT `June 2, 2026`
- Time format: `14:00` or `2pm`, NOT `2:00 PM`
- Currency: `£` symbol before number, e.g. `£45` not `45£`
- Apostrophes: `it's = it is`, `its = possessive` (most common error)
- Ellipsis: three dots, no space before, optional space after

## Stylistic UK conventions (flag only if obviously American)

- Vocabulary: `pavement` (not sidewalk), `queue` (not line), `chemist` or `pharmacy` (not drugstore), `petrol` (not gas), `holiday` (not vacation)
- Polite distance: UK English tends slightly more formal than US in marketing copy

## The four passes

**Pass 1: Spelling.** Identify every US spelling that should be UK. Identify every misspelling regardless of variant.

**Pass 2: Grammar.** Subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun clarity, "less" vs "fewer", "that" vs "which", dangling modifiers.

**Pass 3: Punctuation.** Em or en dashes get a HARD FLAG (always cite the CLAUDE.md rule). Apostrophe errors (it's/its, your/you're), comma splices, quotation marks, date/time/currency format consistency.

**Pass 4: Stylistic (optional, low-priority).** Obvious American vocabulary in UK context. AI-tell phrases ("delve into", "navigate the complexities", "in today's fast-paced world"). Filler words that weaken sentences ("very", "really", "absolutely" used without intent).
