## 3. Sections in order - answers MECHANISM and CONFIDENCE

For each section, provide:
- **Section name**
- **Conversion sequence role** (Mechanism / Confidence)
- **Purpose** (what this section does for the visitor)
- **Content brief** (3 to 5 bullets)
- **CTA in this section** (if any)

### Section 1: Problem framing
**Sequence role:** Relevance reinforced + Mechanism intro
**Purpose:** Visitor recognises themselves in the description of the problem.
**Content brief:**
- Pain or trigger described in the visitor's words (from persona)
- Validate that this is a common / solvable issue
- Bridge to the service
**CTA:** [Optional - first CTA opportunity for high-intent visitors]

### Section 2: What this service is
**Sequence role:** Mechanism
**Purpose:** Plain English explanation of the service, who it's for, what happens.
**Content brief:**
- The service in one sentence
- Who it's right for (qualifying language)
- Who it's NOT for (light, but valuable)
- What you get / what changes
**CTA:** [Optional secondary CTA]

### Section 3: What happens (process)
**Sequence role:** Mechanism + Confidence
**Purpose:** Demystify the experience. Reduce uncertainty.
**Content brief:**
- 3 to 4 steps with brief description each
- Time / duration / cost transparency
- What the visitor brings, what we provide

### Section 4: Trust and proof
**Sequence role:** Confidence
**Purpose:** Move visitor from interest to readiness.
**Content brief:**
- Highest-power trust signals here (testimonials with photos, named outcomes)
- Credentials and recognition (GMC for Stride, England Golf for Fade, etc.)
- Press or local recognition
- For Stride: CQC registration link

### Section 5: Risk reversal
**Sequence role:** Confidence (the close-the-gap section)
**Purpose:** Reduce perceived risk of saying yes.
**Content brief:**
- Free consultation / no-commitment trial / money-back / cancel anytime
- What guarantee or reassurance applies
- Friction-reducing assurance for the form / call to come
**CTA:** [Primary CTA - second placement]

### Section 6: FAQ (top 4 to 6 objections)
**Sequence role:** Confidence
**Purpose:** Address objections in the visitor's voice, near the final CTA.
**Content brief:**
- Objections from persona Barriers field, framed as visitor questions
- Answers brief, not preachy
- Order objections by frequency, not by length

### Section 7: Final CTA + visible next step
**Sequence role:** Action
**Purpose:** Single clear next step.
**Content brief:**
- Restated value
- Final CTA, same language as primary above-the-fold CTA
- Visible next step ("you'll see availability for next week", "we'll get back to you by tomorrow")

## 5. Trust signals (placed by power, not by space)

Power hierarchy (top = strongest):
1. Named-outcome testimonial with photo
2. Specific numbers with context
3. Press / local recognition
4. Credentials / professional bodies
5. Generic testimonials
6. Star ratings

Placement:
- ONE in the first screen (above-the-fold) - highest power available
- Two to four in Section 4 (Trust and proof)
- One in Section 7 (Final CTA section) if available

| Signal | Power tier | Page location |
|---|---|---|
| [e.g. "Sarah, 42: 'Walking again pain-free after 3 weeks'" with photo] | 1 | Above-the-fold |
| [e.g. "200+ patients treated, average 4.8 stars from 87 reviews"] | 2 | Section 4 |
| [e.g. "GMC-registered clinicians, CQC-registered clinic"] | 4 | Footer + Section 4 |
| [e.g. "Featured in Leamington Courier, May 2026"] | 3 | Section 4 |

**Stride-specific:** Any clinical claim needs Harry sign-off. Flag in the brief if any trust signal makes a clinical outcome claim.

**Form purpose:** [booking / lead capture / call request]

| Field | Required | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Name | Yes | Routing - need to address them |
| Email or phone | One required, prefer phone | Contact for booking confirmation |
| [Specific question] | Yes | Changes what happens next |

**Form-field rule**: every field answers ONE question - does this change what happens next? If no, cut the field.

**Form mechanics:**
- Mobile-first labels (16px+ text, 48x48px tap targets)
- Inline validation, not modal errors
- One question per "screen" if long form
- Privacy notice link visible above submit
- Marketing opt-in: granular, separate tick-box, not bundled into submit

## 9. UK compliance pass

### Cookies (PECR / ICO 2026)
- Cookie banner present and PECR-compliant
- Accept All and Reject All buttons with equal visual prominence (ICO 2026 update - this is now an explicit requirement)
- No pre-ticked consent boxes
- Granular per-category control available

### GDPR (forms)
- Privacy notice link visible above submit button
- Lawful basis for processing stated: contract (booking) or consent (marketing)
- Marketing opt-in granular tick-box, separate from booking submission, not pre-ticked
- Data retention period referenced if relevant

### CQC compliance (Stride pages only)
- CQC registration number displayed in footer
- Link to CQC profile or inspection report visible
- All clinical claims evidence-based - any specific outcome claim flagged for Harry sign-off
- No "guarantee" language on clinical outcomes
- Practitioner credentials displayed (GMC / HCPC / professional body)

### Accessibility (cross-references [[Visual Brand Check]])
- WCAG 2.2 AA: contrast 4.5:1 (text), 3:1 (large text and UI components)
- Alt text on all images
- Keyboard navigable
- Form labels properly associated

## 10. One thing to test next

**Default mode: before-and-after, not formal A/B test.** Most Maddie pages don't have the traffic for a statistically valid A/B test. Propose one named variant worth trying after the page has 4 weeks of baseline conversion data.

**Variable to try:** [ONE element - headline, primary CTA, hero image, form length, or risk-reversal language]

**Why this one:** [one sentence from persona / framework reasoning]

**How to compare:** [4 weeks pre vs 4 weeks post on the same primary conversion metric. Note that the result is directional, not statistically valid - useful for "this clearly helped / hurt", not for marginal calls.]

**When formal A/B testing applies:** Only for high-volume Stride paid-search pages (>500 conversions / month). In that case: hypothesis, single variable, 95% confidence, sample-size calc. Default to before-and-after for everything else.
