# Audience Persona, Worked Example

A worked example of the Audience Persona Builder running end-to-end on Stride orthotics, new prospects, for the July relaunch campaign. Shows Maddie-first input, research triangulation, and the follow-on opportunity surfacing pattern in action.

```
Maddie: build persona for stride orthotics

Skill: Brand confirmed Stride, service confirmed orthotics. Is this for general reference, or for the July relaunch campaign? And is the persona for new prospects, existing patients, or both?

Maddie: the relaunch, new prospects

Skill: OK. Stride currently has 3 personas across the brand. Adding orthotics gets us to 4, within the healthy range. Proceeding.

Question 1: Job-to-be-done. When someone hires Stride for orthotics, what job are they trying to get done? Three layers: practical, feeling they want, how they want to be seen.

Maddie: Practical, stop the knee or foot pain that is interrupting training. Feeling, they want to feel like a serious athlete again, not a patient. Socially, they want to be the friend who got it fixed and kept running, not the one who gave up.

Skill: That is rich, all three layers usable. Question 2: behaviour-led description. Lead with what is going on in their life when they decide to book. Demographics as supporting detail.

Maddie: They have been training through pain for weeks or months, usually have a race or event coming up, getting nervous they will not start or finish. Tried new shoes, foam rolling, rest weeks. They are 25 to 50, runners and footballers, often work desk jobs around their training, live within 30 minutes of the clinic.

Skill: Good, behaviour leads, demographics support. Question 3: Voice of Customer. Give me 3 to 5 real phrases you have heard from these patients.

Maddie: "I just want to keep running." "I have a marathon in 8 weeks and I am panicking." "I have spent a fortune on new shoes already." "My physio said it might be my arches but I do not really know what that means."

Skill: Those are gold. Question 4: success factors. What does winning look like 3 months after they book?

Maddie: Back to full training volume. No pain on long runs. Hit the event they were aiming for. Started recommending Stride to other runners in their club.

Skill: Specific and measurable. Question 5: decision criteria. What do they compare across providers?

Maddie: Speed of appointment (NHS waiting list is 18 weeks here). Whether the practitioner runs themselves, big credibility signal. Cost, they want to know upfront. Reviews from other athletes, not just general patients. Whether we make custom orthotics or just off-the-shelf.

Skill: Question 6: triggers? Question 7: barriers? Question 8: information-seeking? Question 9: anti-persona? Question 10: secondary?

Maddie: 
Triggers: pain interrupts a planned event, friend recommendation, finally Googling at 11pm after a bad run.
Barriers: cost, "is this actually going to work" doubt, stigma that orthotics are old-people stuff.
Information-seeking: late-night Google sessions on phone, lurking in local running club Facebook groups before asking, GP referral attempts then frustration with waiting list.
Anti-persona: people looking for free NHS-style consultation, casual walkers who want generic insoles, anyone wanting a "rest for 6 weeks" diagnosis.
Secondary: older patients 60+ with diabetic foot issues, GP-referred, prevention not performance motivation. Very different persona, deserves its own file eventually.

Skill: Excellent. Running research pass now to triangulate. 4 search angles.

[Skill runs Quick Research, returns 4 sources cited]

Skill: Research mostly confirms. Three additions worth noting:
1. NHS waiting times for podiatry orthotics referrals confirmed at 18 to 22 weeks in Warwickshire. Private speed is a documented differentiator.
2. The "orthotics are for old people" stigma is widely documented across endurance sports, also applies to cycling. Wider than just running.
3. Decision criteria research highlights "before-and-after photos of real patients" as the single highest-trust signal for athletic patients evaluating podiatry clinics. Add to Trust Signals.

Drafting the persona file with all 14 fields populated. Want to see it before I save?

Maddie: yes

Maddie: looks strong, only thing is on Decision Criteria I would put "practitioner credibility" higher than I said earlier. That is the number one thing if cost is roughly comparable.

[shows revised section]

- Secondary persona (older diabetic patients) is a separate brief for a separate campaign, do not mix the two.
```
