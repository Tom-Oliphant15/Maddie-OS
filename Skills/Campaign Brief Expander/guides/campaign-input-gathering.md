# Campaign Input Gathering (Step 3 of Campaign Brief Expander)

The ten campaign-specific inputs to gather from Maddie AFTER Brain context is confirmed (Step 2 of [[Campaign Brief Expander]]). Each sub-step includes the question to ask, examples of strong answers, and explicit push-back patterns for weak answers. The skill applies critical-persona discipline throughout: if any input is weak, surface the gap before producing the plan. Do not produce a plan around a flawed brief.

---

ONLY after Brain context is confirmed. Ask these, in order. Push back hard where the answer is generic.

## 3.1. Background / Why now
What is the business context for this campaign? What has changed (seasonality, supply, competitor move, internal capacity)? Why is this the right moment? Two or three sentences.

**Push back if:** "to grow the business" or other generic. Force specificity: "we just finished training Wendy and have orthotic capacity for the first time in 4 months" beats "to grow".

## 3.2. Primary Goal (the one number)
One specific, measurable, dated outcome.

**Push back if:** vague. Examples of strong goals:
- "30 booked nail surgery consultations in June"
- "Luke ambassador launch generating 50 membership enquiries within 4 weeks"
- "300 leaflet QR scans from F&E door drop converted to 15 trial bookings"

## 3.3. Supporting KPIs (2 to 3 max)
Leading indicators that tell us we are on track BEFORE the primary goal lands.

**Examples:**
- CTR ≥ 3% on Google Ads
- Cost per conversion ≤ £15
- Engagement uplift ≥ 20% on the campaign posts vs the previous 4-week average
- Page views to /nail-surgery from the campaign ≥ 500

**Push back if:** Maddie names >3 KPIs. Per the research, more than 3 makes them no longer "key" and confuses execution. If she has 5 candidates, ask her to rank and pick the top 3.

## 3.4. Key Messages (2 to 4 max)
The messages every asset in this campaign must communicate. Designers, videographers, and Maddie herself work from this list. Stops cross-asset drift.

**Example for Stride nail surgery:**
1. We do nail surgery in Leamington / Warwick, same-week appointments available
2. Permanent solution, not a temporary fix
3. Friendly local podiatrists who understand this is uncomfortable to deal with

**Push back if:** more than 4 messages, or messages overlap. Force priority.

## 3.5. Deadline + key milestones
Not just one date.

- Campaign go-live date
- Peak / launch weekend
- Mid-campaign re-emphasis date (if running >2 weeks)
- Wind-down / review date

**Push back if:** "soon", "next month-ish".

## 3.6. Budget allocation
If there is paid spend, break it down (not lump sum).

- Google Ads: £X
- Boosted social: £X
- Print / leaflets: £X
- Talent / commission costs: £X
- Contingency: £X (recommend 10-15%)

Total must reconcile.

**Push back if:** Maddie gives a lump sum and tells me to allocate. Refuse: "Allocation drives decisions later: what to pause, what to double on. You should own this. Want me to suggest a starting split based on the goal + KPIs?"

## 3.7. Stakeholders + Approval gates
Who signs off on what, by when.

- Strategy sign-off: [Tom / Harry / Tom + Harry], by date
- Creative sign-off (assets ready to publish): [usually Maddie + reviewing director], by date
- Clinical / compliance sign-off (Stride only): Harry or named clinician, before any clinical claim or patient content goes out
- Patient consent (Stride only): Wendy confirms consent on file before patient-facing content publishes
- Spend approval threshold: any spend above £X needs Harry / Tom approval

**Push back if:** Maddie names herself as sole approver on everything. Force "where does Tom + Harry need to see this before live?"

## 3.8. Reference material + past campaigns
The skill has already loaded the Campaign Archive. Show Maddie what was found:

```
Past campaigns for Stride nail surgery in the Archive:
- [2026-03-15] Stride Nail Surgery March, hook "what 90% of people try first", 22% engagement uplift, 18 booked consultations. Winner.
- [2026-01-22] Stride Foot Health January, generic "look after your feet" hook, 3% engagement uplift, 4 booked consultations. Underperformed.
```

Ask Maddie: "Want this brief to draw on the March pattern (winner) and explicitly avoid the January pattern? Yes / refine / ignore archive."

If Archive is empty (first campaign for this brand + service): surface honestly. The brief will be built without historical reference, and the Archive populates as future campaigns complete + Data Pull cycles run.

## 3.9. Out of scope (explicit)
What is the campaign NOT doing? Prevents scope creep.

**Example:** "Not doing: TikTok content (we are not active on TikTok for Stride yet). Not doing: paid Instagram (organic only on IG, paid lives on Google Ads only). Not doing: chasing GP referrals (separate B2B workstream, parallel)."

**Push back if:** Maddie says "we will do whatever performs". Force discipline. The brief is the boundary.

## 3.10. Kill criteria
Decision rules for pulling the plug or pivoting mid-campaign.

**Example:** "If by end of week 1 we have <5 booked consultations OR CTR <1.5%, pause Google Ads spend, review hook, may pivot to a different angle. If end-week-2 booking count is on track, double down on the winning headline + description pair for the mid-campaign re-emphasis."

**Push back if:** Maddie has no kill criteria. Without them, underperforming campaigns burn budget for the full window.

---

**Apply critical persona throughout.** If any of 3.1 to 3.10 is weak, surface the gap before producing the plan. Do not produce a plan around a flawed brief.
