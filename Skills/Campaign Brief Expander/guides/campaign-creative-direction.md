# Campaign Creative Direction Patterns

Reference patterns for Section 15 of the [[Campaign Brief Expander]] output. Hook formulas, caption structure, platform-specific format guidance, CTA per platform, UGC, posting cadence. Drawn from social media research and Maddie's voice reference posts. The brief uses these to guide creative direction without writing the actual post copy.

---

## Hook angles to test (drawn from voice reference posts + past performance archive + current trends)

Use one of these proven hook formulas per asset, generate 10 hook candidates per asset, pick the strongest:

- **Specific Claim:** open with a precise number, evidence, or research finding. Specificity signals authority. ("After 200 nail surgeries we know what 90% of patients tried first that did not work...")
- **Relatable Problem:** open by naming a problem the audience experiences and usually does not say out loud. Recognition is a powerful scroll-stopper. ("You stopped wearing sandals two summers ago, and you have not told anyone why.")
- **Open Loop:** start a story and deliberately do not resolve it in the first two lines. Curiosity pulls them past the read-more cut-off. ("Last June a patient came in saying she would never wear shoes properly again. Six weeks later...")
- **Counter-intuitive:** state something that contradicts what the audience expects to hear. Pattern-break stops the scroll. ("Most ingrown toenails do not need surgery. The ones that do, you cannot fix yourself.")

Lock 3 to 5 angles to test in week 1 of the campaign. Double down on the winner from week 2 onwards.

## Caption structure (every social asset)

Three-part structure, scannable, optimised for platform truncation:

1. **Hook (line 1-2, before read-more cuts off):** specific, audience-recognising. This is what stops the scroll.
2. **Value (lines 3-6, ≤2 lines per paragraph):** the substance. Educate, not sell. For health and wellness brands education is the highest-leverage content type. The audience builds trust before they buy.
3. **CTA (final line):** specific action, audience language, never "click here". "Book your free consultation," "Save this for the next time it flares," "DM if it sounds like you."

Break any paragraph longer than 2 to 3 lines. Visual scannability matters as much as the words.

## Platform-specific format guidance (current as of latest [[Reviews/Social Trends/]] report)

- **Instagram Reels:** 15-45 seconds optimal. Vertical 9:16. Hook in first 1-2 seconds (the muted-preview moment). Algorithm prioritises watch-time + saves + comments.
- **TikTok:** 15-30 seconds optimal. Vertical 9:16. Trending audio is still a meaningful boost when relevant. Comments per video average ~54 (highest engagement platform).
- **YouTube Shorts:** 15-35 seconds optimal. Vertical 9:16. Comments much lower (~20 per video), but discovery is stronger.
- **Instagram main feed:** square 1:1 or vertical 4:5. Carousel posts outperform single images for save-rate.
- **Facebook:** longer captions tolerated (older demographic, more reading). First 1-2 lines still critical.

## CTA per platform

- **Instagram:** link in bio + DM trigger ("DM 'NAIL' for the booking link")
- **Facebook:** direct booking link, longer copy can include URL inline
- **TikTok:** comment-driven CTA ("comment 'YES' for the booking link", TikTok algorithm rewards comments)
- **Google Ads:** phone + book-online buttons enabled

## User-generated content (where applicable)

For health and wellness specifically, UGC outperforms polished brand content. People want to see real results from real people who face similar issues. For Stride patient testimonials:
- Real patient on camera (consent confirmed by Wendy)
- Phone-shot is fine, polish is not the goal
- Trust comes from imperfection, not production value

---

## Posting cadence (current best practice)

3 to 5 posts per week per active platform is the sustainable sweet spot for small brands. Daily Stories supplement the main feed without burning content stock. Multi-platform posting (3+ platforms with platform-appropriate variants, not identical cross-posts) generates 4 to 5x the reach of single-platform.
