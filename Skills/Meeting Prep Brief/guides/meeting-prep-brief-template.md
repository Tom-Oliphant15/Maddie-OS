## 3. Topics to raise (your agenda)

Maximum 8 main points. Each topic framed as a question or a decision-needed, not a topic label. Glanceable in a live meeting.

For each topic, mark its type:
- **D** = Decision needed today
- **A** = Ask of attendee(s)
- **U** = Update to give

Example structure:

1. **[D] Should we commit Stride's June budget to YouTube or hold for paid search?** [one-line context, sub-bullet of data backing the question]
2. **[A] Can Harry unblock WordPress access by Friday?** [why it's blocking, what the workaround has been]
3. **[U] Master Marketing Document drafting status.** [where it is, what's left, blocker if any]
[...]

Rules:
- Question-framed topics ("Should we...", "Can you...", "Are we still on for...") sharpen better than label-framed ("Stride budget", "WordPress", "Master doc"). Always frame as a question or decision-needed.
- Maximum 8. If more, the meeting cannot land it all and the brief should cut to the most important 8.
- Each topic carries its type tag (D/A/U). Helps Maddie pace the meeting; helps the agenda template in Section 4 allocate time.

### 3a. What they'll likely raise (anticipation block)

**Only run for Format type = four-way or external partner.** Skip for 1:1 and three-way recurring meetings.

For four-way and external partner meetings, list the 2 to 4 things the other side will likely raise, with Maddie's draft answer:

| They'll likely raise | Your draft answer |
|---|---|
| [thing] | [one-line position] |

Sources for anticipation: previous meeting summary, recent Slack/email threads pulled in Step 2, the active campaign or contract the meeting touches.

### 3b. Out of scope today (kill list)

Things that may come up but are NOT for this meeting. Helps Maddie say "let's park that" without losing the agenda.

1. [item to park]
2. [item to park]
[Max 3. If none, skip the section.]

## 4. Suggested agenda (with timings)

For a [N]-minute meeting:

| Time | Item | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00, 5 min | Last meeting actions check-in | [whoever] |
| 0:05, 10 min | [Topic 1, question form] | [whoever drives] |
| 0:15, 10 min | [Topic 2, question form] | [whoever drives] |
| 0:25, 5 min | Next steps, action owners | [chair] |

**Time-box check.** Sum of allocated topic time should leave 5 minutes for next steps. If `(topics x realistic minutes) > (slot length - 5 min)`, cut topics or split into a follow-up. Surface to Maddie: "Agenda runs [N] minutes over. Cut which topic, or schedule a follow-up?"

**Agenda weight by meeting type:**
- Decision: bulk of time on the decision itself, status walk-through is minimal (read the brief).
- Problem-solving: weight on the problem, light on status.
- Update: weight on status walk-through, action items at end.
- Alignment: weight on the alignment topic, decisions parked unless trivial.
- Mixed: split proportionally.
