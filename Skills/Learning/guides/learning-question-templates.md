# Learning, Question templates per section type

These are the templates the skill uses when asking Maddie to fill a gap. Each is a specific, answerable question (never an open prompt like "tell me about X"). All questions are wrapped in the Learning skill's standard preamble (What this is / Why it matters / Current state) before being asked.

---

## For audience persona, Primary persona

> For [brand]'s [service] audience, who is the typical person you see?
>
> Specifically:
> - Age range and gender skew (e.g. "40 to 65, 60% female")
> - Where they live (e.g. "Leamington and within 20 min drive")
> - What is going on in their life when they decide to book (e.g. "tried home remedies for months, finally fed up")
> - What stops them booking immediately (e.g. "worried it will hurt", "worried about cost")
>
> Two or three sentences is enough. I will format it for the Brain file.

## For audience persona, Watering Holes

> Where does [brand]'s [service] audience spend time online or offline?
>
> Examples:
> - Specific Facebook groups (local community pages, special-interest groups)
> - Specific subreddits or forums
> - Local in-person spots (parkruns, school gates, gyms, GP waiting rooms)
> - Search behaviour patterns (late night Googling? Specific terms?)
>
> Three to five concrete places is enough.

## For brand voice, Tone

> How would you describe [brand]'s tone in 2 to 3 sentences?
>
> Think about: how it should feel to read, what mood it sets, what it is NOT.
>
> Example (Stride): "Warm and clinical. Reassuring without being patronising. Plain English over medical jargon when speaking to patients. NOT cold-clinical, NOT overly casual."

## For brand guidelines, Colour palette

> Tell me [brand]'s colour palette.
>
> For each colour, give me the name and the hex code (you can pull these from Canva's brand kit, or from the Master Marketing Document, or just the colour names if you do not have hex codes yet).
>
> I need:
> - Primary (the main brand colour)
> - Secondary (the second-most-used)
> - Accent (used sparingly for emphasis)
> - Backgrounds (off-whites or neutrals you use behind content)
> - Avoid (any colours that drift the brand, e.g. "harsh clinical-cold blue" for Stride)
>
> If you do not know the hex codes, paste 3 example designs and I can sample the colours from them.

## For brand guidelines, Typography

> Which fonts does [brand] use?
>
> - Headlines: which font + weight (e.g. "Georgia Bold")
> - Body: which font (often a different one from headlines)
> - Display / decorative: any font used for posters, banners, or hero shots only
> - Avoid: any font you have seen used that looked wrong (we add these to a drift-catcher list)
>
> If you do not know exact font names, describe what they look like (serif / sans-serif, light / heavy) and I will help match.

---

## For brand guidelines, Logo

> A few logo questions in one go:
>
> - **Where is the logo required?** (e.g. "every social post", "leaflets only", "only on website")
> - **Where is the logo optional or forbidden?**
> - **Clear space:** how much empty space must surround the logo (often expressed as "minimum 1x logo height of clear space on all sides")
> - **Minimum size:** smallest the logo can be without losing recognition (e.g. "never smaller than 32px digital, 20mm print")
> - **Colour variants:** is there a white-on-dark version, dark-on-white, monochrome?

---

## For brand guidelines, Imagery style

> Tell me how [brand] uses imagery.
>
> - **Use:** what kind of imagery feels on-brand (e.g. for Stride: "real photos of clinic, team, or patients with consent. Soft natural light.")
> - **Avoid:** what kind of imagery drifts the brand (e.g. "stock illustrations of feet/legs", "anonymous medical team-in-scrubs")
> - **Composition preferences:** angles, framing, lighting

>

## For stub-state skill

Used when a skill file is flagged as stub-state (e.g. Voice Notes, Cross-Platform Repurposer, Data Pull). The goal is NOT to fill in the skill — that's impossible while the upstream blocker is unresolved. The goal is to make the blocker concrete enough that Tom + Maddie can move it through to resolution rather than letting it sit indefinitely.

> The **[skill name]** skill is currently a stub. The changelog says it's blocked on **[blocker as written in the skill file]**.
>
> Three quick questions so we can log this properly in your Open Questions registry:
>
> - **Who owns the decision** that unblocks this? (You, Tom, both of you, a third party?)
> - **What concretely needs to happen** before the skill can be built out? (A software pick, an account setup, a contract, a meeting with someone, an external tool launch?)
> - **When could it realistically land?** (This week, this month, this quarter, "no current view"?)
>
> I'll write the answers into `Information/Open Questions.md` with status "Blocked". Each morning your Daily Briefing will surface it as one of the open items until it gets resolved or the ETA passes. That way the stub stops being invisible and starts being a thing we can actually move.

After Maddie answers, write a row to `Information/Open Questions.md` and confirm with her in one sentence: "Logged. Daily Briefing will surface this each morning until [Owner] confirms it's resolved."

## For Key People, role-level verification

> When you mentioned [Name] earlier, you described their role as "[role]". I want to make sure I'm recording their **actual level** in the hierarchy, not just a generic title.
>
> Are they a:
> - Therapist (clinical / treatment staff)
> - Clinical Lead (senior clinician at a clinic)
> - Practice Manager (operational lead at a clinic)
> - Area Manager (covers more than one clinic)
> - Director (above Area Manager)
> - Something else (PT tenant, ambassador, contractor, partner — different category entirely)
>
> The reason this matters: a Clinical Lead recorded as "Therapist" will get the wrong tone in any draft the OS produces for them, and the wrong routing in meeting prep.
