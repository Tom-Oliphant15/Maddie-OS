# Guide: Platform-Native Framing and Script Architecture

Reference detail for [[Filming Brief Generator]]. Extracted from the skill file 2026-05-21 to keep the skill lean. Covers the 9:16-first framing rules, secondary crops, and the Hook to Problem to Solution to CTA script architecture every talking-talent shoot uses.

---

## Platform-Native Framing

Default: **9:16 vertical, 1080x1920**.

Cover natively in this aspect ratio: TikTok, Instagram Reels and Stories, YouTube Shorts, Facebook Reels and Stories, LinkedIn vertical feed, Pinterest Video Pins. Most short-form distribution happens here.

Secondary crops (derived from the 9:16 master):
- **1:1 (1080x1080)**: Instagram feed (deprecated for video on most accounts but useful for feed-grid consistency).
- **16:9 (1920x1080)**: YouTube long-form, website embed, in-clinic / in-venue screens.

Rule: frame the master shot in 9:16 with "headroom and footroom" for safe cropping to 1:1 and 16:9. Important action and faces in the centre 60% of the frame. Captions positioned in the safe zone (centre-vertical, not top or bottom edge where platform UI overlays sit).

Shoot in 4K where possible to allow for re-framing and zooming in post.

## Hook and Script Architecture

Every speaking-talent shoot uses Hook to Problem to Solution to CTA structure.

### Hook (first 3 seconds)

The hook does ONE of:
- **States the outcome.** "This is how I got back on the course after surgery."
- **Names the problem in the viewer's words.** "If your knee gives out on the 4th hole, watch this."
- **Pattern interrupt.** Visual or verbal jolt that breaks the scroll (close-up start, unexpected statement, mid-action open).
- **Curiosity opening.** "Most people get this wrong about ingrown toenails."

NOT acceptable: brand intro ("Hi, I'm Luke from Fade Golf"), slow setup, throat-clearing, "today I want to talk about". Viewers will scroll before you finish your name.

The hook is named explicitly in the brief, with the exact opening line.

### Problem (3 to 8 seconds)

The pain or trigger validated. Make the viewer feel "this is for me". In the viewer's words, from the persona Triggers field.

### Solution (8 to 30 seconds)

What we do / how it works / proof. This is the middle of the script. Pace with cuts every 2 to 4 seconds.

### CTA (last 5 seconds)

Specific, value-exchange CTA:
- "Tap the link for free 15-minute assessment."
- "Code FADE20 at the link in our bio."
- "DM us 'TRIAL' for a free week."

NOT "follow for more", "link in bio", "check us out".

### Retention rules

- Cuts every 2 to 4 seconds.
- Alternate wide / medium / close shots. No two same-frame shots in a row.
- Never stay on one shot longer than 3 spoken sentences.
- Burned-in captions present from second 0, drives the silent-view audience to retain.
